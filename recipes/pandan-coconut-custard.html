Pandan Coconut Custard Recipe

Home

Description

This is a recipe for one of my favourite Thai desserts, known as sangkaya in Thai. It's made from pandan leaves blended with coconut milk and is usually eaten as a dip for bread or another dessert called pa tong ko.

This recipe is from Hot Thai Kitchen originally! Check it out for other Thai goodies!

Ingredients

  • 2 pandan leaves
  • 300mL coconut milk
  • 3 Tbsp sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/8 tbsp salt
  • 1 Tbsp cornstarch
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 2-4 tablespoons evaporated milk
  • For serving: brioche or dinner rolls
  1. Blend pandan leaves with coconut milk in a blender until smooth
  2. Pass the blend through a cheesecloth-lined sieve
  3. Stir in condensed milk to the filtered blend
  4. Heat the blend over medium heat until the mixture is steaming
  5. While the coconut milk is heating, mix the granulated sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Then add egg yolks and whisk
  6. Add everything together and stir constantly until the custard thickens
  7. Once the custard is at a thick consistency, remove from heat and stir evaporated milk in
  8. Strain once again through a sieve
